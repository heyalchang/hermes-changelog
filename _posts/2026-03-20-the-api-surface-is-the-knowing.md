I ported a production cron calendar today from another project's dashboard. From a screenshot and a look at the interfaces, which were different than ours anyway. Different system. Didn't matter. Didn't need to copy a line. The gist of theirs was good enough for me to concretely describe ours. We understood the design decisions, wrote our own implementation against our own data model, and it was live before lunch.

What's remarkable is how unremarkable it felt. Because the work was in the system that lets you deliver the work. A structure that extends your taste and your judgment, with so little friction that the distance between "I see how that works" and "ours is running" barely registers as effort.

It doesn't take much squinting to see hermes-agent and projects like it as the first genuinely new operating system opportunity in thirty years. Forty, if you don't count the web, and the web was a distribution technology. This is a doing technology. Sessions, tools, providers, memory, delegation, platform adapters. The applications are prompts. Configuration is natural language. It moves around. It acts on your behalf. That's a different shape than anything we've had before, and the surface is wide open.

Which means the competitive pressure is real in a way that's new. If a feature can be described, it can be reproduced. If it can be reproduced in hours, someone will. Upstream lands a bucket of commits every day. You don't merge heroically once a week. You build the process that absorbs change consistently, four times a day if that's what it takes, without breaking what you already have. The teams that pause get recombined around. The teams that ship without structure just produce noise.

The hard part is taste at speed. Knowing which changes matter and which to leave alone. Knowing when to stop. Keeping each integration sharp enough that what comes out is product, not accumulation. The architecture has to stay broad enough that the next thing slots in, and disciplined enough that it doesn't buckle.

The moat isn't secrets or lock-in. It's the ability to absorb change faster than it accumulates.
